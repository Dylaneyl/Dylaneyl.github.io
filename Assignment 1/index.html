What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When I first opened the webpage, I encountered a simple orange screen with artistic scribbling unfolding before my eyes. The initial thing that caught my attention was a prompt to enable sound by clicking on this screen. The sound prompt closely followed my cursor, highlighting the significance of audio in the experience. Additionally, the word 'story' on the landing page indicated that I was about to start a visual and auditory journey unlike a typical website in which i was expecting.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Upon loading the webpage, my first action was clicking to enable sound. I then noticed text following my cursor, so I moved it back and forth across the screen while trying to read the sentences following my mouse. As I explored the landing page by moving my cursor around, the 'Sculpting Harmony' heading stretched and distorted. This prompted me to navigate down the webpage by scrolling. For the rest of the two minutes, I continued scrolling as I watched the story unfold.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The majority of my time was spent on the landing page, where I kept moving the cursor back and forth while attempting to read the sentences that followed my mouse. After approximately a minute of doing this, I realised the words were repeating and to my disappointment, lacked meaningful content.

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

During the brief two minute interaction I had with the website, the most common action was scrolling. After the introduction on the landing page, the story progressed smoothly as I scrolled down the page at my own pace. The elements of the webpage are intertwined with the scrolling action, and the only way to progress the experience is by scrolling down.

The primary goal of this interactive experience is to depict how Frank Gehry and his team designed the Walt Disney concert hall in Los Angeles. It runs through the inspiration, drafting process, design choices and construction. The interactive experience also acts as a tourism piece encouraging people to visit the monument when in LA.

The interactive experience effectively communicates its primary goal by providing users with small pieces of information as they navigate down the page. As users scroll, details about Gehry and his team's creation process for the Walt Disney Concert Hall are provided. The inclusion of glowing reviews about the venue seems to add persuasive element, aiming to entice users to visit the concert hall.
