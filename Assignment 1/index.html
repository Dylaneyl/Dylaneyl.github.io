How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

During the brief two minute interaction I had with the website, the most common action was scrolling. After the introduction on the landing page, the story progressed smoothly as I scrolled down the page at my own pace. The elements of the webpage are intertwined with the scrolling action, and the only way to progress the experience is by scrolling down.

The primary goal of this interactive experience is to depict how Frank Gehry and his team designed the Walt Disney concert hall in Los Angeles. It runs through the inspiration, drafting process, design choices and construction. The interactive experience also acts as a tourism piece encouraging people to visit the monument when in LA.

The interactive experience effectively communicates its primary goal by providing users with small pieces of information as they navigate down the page. As users scroll, details about Gehry and his team's creation process for the Walt Disney Concert Hall are provided. The inclusion of glowing reviews about the venue seems to add persuasive element, aiming to entice users to visit the concert hall.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The interactive website is intended as a one time experience and it took me about 5 minutes to navigate through the story. It is intended to be viewed before or after visiting the Walt Disney concert hall, as it adds perspective on the creation of the LA monument.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The website tells a story with a beginning and a conclusion. This conveys to the user that once they've reached the end there is no more to see. It has chapters similar to a novel, and doesn't provide users with a reason to return once they’ve experienced it for the first time. The website doesn’t have a shop or any kind of monetisation, which strengthens the theory that its only intended to be viewed once.
